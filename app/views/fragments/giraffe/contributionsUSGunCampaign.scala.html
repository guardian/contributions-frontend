Meaningful solutions to America's gun violence epidemic are getting lost in a cycle of despair and outrage that define the media's response to gun tragedies. And nothing ever changes as a result.

At the Guardian, we do not accept the orthodoxy that the debate about gun control is simply too difficult. Our new series, Break the Cycle, will challenge the way the media covers gun violence. It will drive an ongoing conversation about possible solutions, and hold politicians to account for ignoring reforms that could save lives.

To drive change in America, we need to recognize that gun rights advocates are important partners — and that citizens on all sides want to make the country safer. We will address gun violence in all its forms: not just mass shootings, but the more than 100 people killed daily in suicides, domestic violence, and accidental killings, and the disproportionate burden of gun violence on communities of color.

By keeping the issue of gun violence in the public eye outside of major tragedies, we can keep the US and the world informed and create meaningful change.

We're asking our readers to join us in this effort. The Guardian aims to raise $100,000 to support Break the Cycle. Your support will fund our in-depth reporting on guns throughout 2018.
