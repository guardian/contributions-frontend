America is failing mothers.
Help us change that.

Support the Guardian's new project
on motherhood in America

The Guardian is seeking contributions to fund The Mother Load, a four-month global reporting project to explore why it’s harder to be a mother in America than in any other developed country – and what we can do about it.

The Mother Load will use the Guardian's global perspective to illustrate the many ways the US is an outlier by exploring how our workplaces, politicians, healthcare, wealth distribution, and gender conventions leave mothers facing exceptionally high levels of stress and financial hardship. The less privilege you have, the greater your burden.

But this project isn’t simply about problems – it’s about solutions. We’ll offer advice on how to lobby your workplace for family-friendly policies. We’ll look to other countries for lessons: how did Finland become the only country in the world where men spend more time with their small children than women? And we’ll show how electing more female leaders can directly improve policies for families.

We recognize that fathers are important partners in this battle, and the solutions lie in creating support for families. We’ll strive to show parenthood in all its diversity, sharing perspectives from same-sex couples, single parents, and adoptive families.

We’re asking our readers to fund a four-month reporting project to raise awareness, build community, and inspire activism.

What are we missing? We want to hear from you. We're also seeking larger contributions to support the Guardian's reporting on women and girls from foundations, companies and individuals. If you would like to get involved, please contact us.
